Meeting Mike for the first time
For many of the advanced features of Mike, you need to register with him.  This is fairly easy to do, provided you follow these instructions exactly.  Almost every error is of a PEBKAC nature and can therefore be avoided by RTFM (This document).

The first step is to type /msg Callahan hello
This will introduce you to him, but you're not done yet; there is at least one more command you must utilize, and not doing this ASAP could cause a world of issues, as by not having a password, you will be unable to make any changes later on. This makes for very unhappy users, as based on empirical evidence during the beta stage. We highly recommend a password that you can remember easily, as there is no way to recover it should you forget it.

/msg Callahan PASS <password>

That's all there is!

Teaching Mike your many guises
Mike can remember as many hostmasks for you as he has disk space available. There are two ways to teach him new hostmasks to recognize you with:
IDENT: /msg Callahan IDENT <password>
This is the recommended way if you do not understand what "hostmask" means, and how to determine it and using wildcards to describe it. This
will only add the current hostmask you are using.
ADDHOST: /msg Callahan ADDHOST <password> <hostmask>
This is the more advanced way, as you can add a hostmask that you are not currently using, but know you will use, such as from work, or a shell account. Be careful you don't set too liberal a hostmask; if you use this method, you are responsible for errors arising from it. If you are uncomfortable with this, use IDENT.   The hostmask specified must not match the hostmask of another user.

Our highly recommended solution is to register with CService for undernet. You will need to visit Undernet Channel Service Web Interface, and sign up with a username. Avoid free email accounts such as Yahoo!, or hotmail as many have been disabled for use, due to high abuse levels. Once you've registered with CService, and confirmed, you will need to login when you sign on to IRC, using the following command: /msg X@channels.undernet.org <username> <password>
Once this is done, you can hide your true hostmask, with one of this format: "<username>.users.undernet.org"  by typing /mode <your nick> +x (Mike's is MikeCallahan.users.undernet.org  Please be sure this is the info for the bot you are messaging any passwords to, Mike *can*, and *has* been imitated before) Please note that username does not always mean your nick. The reason this is recommended, is that no matter where you are IRCing from, you can have a consistent hostmask to use. This can be the only hostmask you ever have to tell Mike about, if you do the above steps before saying hello. Many IRC clients have means to do the login and mode commands automatically each time you sign on; check your documentation.

Teaching Mike your personal information
Mike can keep several bits of information about you; currently there are only two, but more will be implemented as time goes on.

TAGLINE: /msg Callahan my tagline is <tagline> /msg Callahan What is|What's my tagline?
To get this information from Mike, there are two commands to use: Mike, who am I? to retrieve your own and Mike, who is <nick>[1] to retrieve other's

USUAL: /msg Callahan my usual is <usual> /msg Callahan What is|What's my usual
This can be used to set your usual "drink" (it can be anything, not necessarily a beverage). This is used in many situations, such as when someone is at the line and you enter, as well as Mike randomly picking patron's usuals for various uses to use in channel.
We do have some advice for syntax in setting this. Mike will usually do something of the nature "Callahan serves Foo <foo's usual>", so we suggest using proper English articles, such as "some", "a/an", "the".  If you're Foo, and you set it by typing /msg Callahan my usual is a heap of lottery money, with a gold bullion chaser it will come out "Callahan serves Foo a heap of lottery money, with a gold bullion chaser."  Also note that Mike added the period; please do not add this yourself, as Mike uses this for other things, and will insert the proper punctuation himself, such as "Callahan serves Foo a heap of lottery money, with a gold bullion chaser, courtesy of Bar".  Note this looks a lot odder if it's "Callahan serves Foo a heap of lottery money, with a gold bullion chaser., courtesy of Bar"[2]

There are several ways to use the usual functions:
Mike, usual (Give a vague request, get a vague answer)
Mike, my usual (Can also be followed by anything else such as "please" or "stat", though he will ignore them)
Mike, give|get me <nicks's> usual[1]
Mike, give|get <nick> my usual
Mike, give|get <nick> <nick's> usual
Mike, give|get <nick> his|their|her usual
Please be sure to use proper possessives, such as "foo's" or "Bars'"[3]

Fly, there's a waiter in my soup!
Mike has an extensive kitchen hidden behind that bar, and it is at your command:
Mike, breakfast|brunch|lunch|dinner|appetizer Mike will pick from the requested menu, and tell you what he recommends.
Mike, snack Mike will pick from a newly installed vending machine, and tell you what he recommends, and its price in varying currencies
Mike, bless me|<nick> Mike serves a God's Blessing.
You can have Mike serve you his last recommendation by telling him Mike, I'll have|take some|one
Mike will remember his last recommendation until someone asks him again. Be daring sometime, and simply ask him for some, and see what turns up!
Mike, chocolate Mike will pick from a chocolate collection, and give you his find. Note you don't have to specifically ask him for it like the above, after all, have you ever seen a chocolate lover refuse?

If you wish to provide either more items for the above, or something new, please post in his forum (Requires registration to post)[4]

/me speaks louder than words
Mike has all sorts of activities you can get him to do.

Mike, tape <nick>  Mike will tape a patron to a random location in the bar. He has many different kinds, and in several glaring color schemes. 133,981 to be exact, if you combine colors, locations, and types of tape.  If you do just colors & types of tape, it's 8,943 combinations of colors & tapes.[1]
Mike, hose <nick>  Mike will hose said person, in various locations on their body. Usually.
Mike, button Mike will grab a button from his never ending supply, and pin it on you.  Currently 494 in the database.
Mike, fortune Mike will grab a fortune cookie, eat the cookie, and leave you with the fortune.  Currently 4,198 in the database.
Mike, unusual Mike will grab something unusual from under the bar, and give it to you.  Currently 39 in the database (Donations welcome!)[4]
Mike, soapbox Mike will get you a soapbox to stand on.  There is no functionality for this like there is for a Toast, nor is there any planned functionality, as this is an informal device.
Mike, play Mike will start XMMS up and play something randomly. Currently 6,881 tracks in the database.

Inquiring minds want to "Oh, no!"
Mike has some features which are more on an informational level, some functional, some not even remotely guaranteed.

Mike, when|how long * Mike will attempt to calculate an answer for you. With expected deviation.
Mike, who * Mike will attempt to say who fits your condition. He's a bit fickle at times, so don't expect consistency.
Mike, should|would|will|shall|could|can|may|is Mike will attempt to squeeze an answer out of Yoda.
Mike, FAQ|rules Mike will display the URL for this page. Tom will spew details in channel.
Mike, help Mike will give the URL for this page. NOTE: This is not the same as messaging Mike for help. There will be an online help function, done by message, that will mostly echo what is here.  It was planned to be implemented for v1.0, but will be instead included in v1.1
Mike, seen <nick> | /msg Callahan seen <nick> Mike will tell you via NOTICE when he last saw that person.
